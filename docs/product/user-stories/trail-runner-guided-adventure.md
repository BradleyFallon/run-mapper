# User Story: Trail Runner, Guided Adventure

Persona:
- [Sam](../personas/sam.md)

## Scenario

Sam wants to try a new trail run, but she does not want to get lost or end up on terrain that is
too muddy, too steep, or too hazardous.

She wants adventure, but not chaos.

## User Goal

She wants a route that feels:
- trail-oriented
- interesting
- somewhat wild
- manageable
- navigable

She wants to avoid:
- terrain that is too muddy
- terrain that is too steep
- too many tripping hazards
- confusing route structure

## Constraints

- wants novelty and nature
- still wants confidence and navigability
- terrain quality matters a lot

## Category, Plan, And Route

- plan category: `Trail`
- starter plan: `Trail Confidence Loop`
- route: a generated trail-focused route that feels adventurous but still legible and within
  terrain tolerances

## Route Preferences

- trail preference over roads
- reasonable steepness ceiling
- better surface conditions
- fewer hazards
- route confidence over pure exploration

## Ideal Product Flow

1. She opens RouteScout and filters plans by the `Trail` category.
2. The app recommends the `Trail Confidence Loop` starter plan.
3. She adjusts trail preference and terrain tolerances.
4. The app generates trail-heavy candidate routes from that plan.
5. The system filters or downranks routes that look too steep, too muddy, or too awkward.
6. The app presents a route that feels adventurous but not reckless.

## What Success Looks Like

She gets a route that feels fresh and natural without the anxiety of:
- getting lost
- taking a wrong turn onto something sketchy
- being surprised by terrain beyond what she wanted

## What The App Must Get Right

- a trustworthy trail plan
- trail detection
- terrain interpretation
- route clarity
- hazard-aware ranking
- confidence messaging

## Product Themes This Story Supports

- controlled adventure
- guided discovery
- terrain-aware route planning
- trail confidence
