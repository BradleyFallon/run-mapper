# User Story: Vacation Landmark Explorer

Persona:
- [Ace](../personas/ace.md)

## Scenario

Ace is on vacation and wants to go for a run as a way of exploring the city.

He is not just trying to get miles in. He wants the run itself to help him see the city and feel
like part of the trip.

## User Goal

He wants a route that is worth doing for what it passes through:
- landmarks
- memorable streets
- parks
- plazas
- waterfronts
- visually interesting neighborhoods

## Constraints

- unfamiliar city
- exploration matters more than technical route perfection
- wants the route to feel curated, not random

## Category, Plan, And Route

- plan category: `Explore`
- starter plan: `City Landmark Run`
- route: a generated city route that connects selected highlights while still feeling runnable and
  easy to follow

## Route Preferences

The route is likely to be driven more by points of interest than by strict training metrics.

Potential preferences:
- landmark density
- scenic quality
- neighborhood character
- route cohesion
- still runnable and easy enough to follow

## Ideal Product Flow

1. He opens RouteScout and filters plans by the `Explore` category.
2. The app recommends the `City Landmark Run` starter plan.
3. The app presents city highlights as cards with photos and descriptions.
4. He ranks or selects the places he most wants to include.
5. The app proposes routes that connect those places sensibly.
6. The final route balances sightseeing with run quality and navigation simplicity.

## What Success Looks Like

The route feels like a guided city experience rather than random mileage.

Ace finishes the run feeling like he:
- saw the city
- discovered places he would not have found on his own
- got a coherent route rather than a chaotic one

## What The App Must Get Right

- a compelling exploration plan
- landmark discovery
- route curation
- route explanation
- visual presentation of highlights
- good balance between exploration and practicality

## Product Themes This Story Supports

- discovery
- delight
- narrative route planning
- sightseeing through running
