# RouteScout Funding Plan Through Release And 1,000 Subscribers

## Purpose

This document defines a founder-facing funding ask from now until:
- the product is released
- the iPhone app exists
- RouteScout reaches `1,000` activated users

The purpose is to produce one clean number to discuss with the co-founder.

## Core Recommendation

Recommended total funding target:
- `$42,000`

This is the number I would recommend presenting as the total funding need from now through:
- build
- launch
- early growth
- developer bonuses through `1,000` activated users

Recommended founder ask:
- the full `$42,000` commitment
- with `$15,000` as the initial deposit, not the whole ask

## Why `$42,000` Is The Right Number

It is large enough to:
- fund the 12-week build phase
- cover basic business and platform costs
- cover light marketing and launch expenses
- reserve the milestone bonuses
- provide a small contingency buffer

It is still modest for the amount of work and scope involved.

## Funding Breakdown

### 1. Developer Pay

- `$12,000`

Assumption:
- `$1,000 / week`
- `12 weeks`

This pays for the initial build phase covering:
- product design
- branding
- web planner development
- iPhone MVP development
- launch execution

### 2. Developer Bonus Reserve

- `$15,000`

Assumption:
- `$5,000` at `100 activated users`
- `$10,000` at `1,000 activated users`

This reserve should be treated as part of the funding plan from the start.

### 3. Platform, Infrastructure, And Service Reserve

- `$500`

This should cover:
- domain registration
- Apple Developer Program fee
- minor admin/software/account costs

### 4. Hosting, Monitoring, And Infrastructure Reserve

- `$1,200`

This should cover:
- app hosting
- backend / database hosting
- monitoring and uptime tools
- deployment infrastructure

### 5. Routing And LLM API Reserve

- `$1,200`

This should cover:
- routing usage
- related geocoding or map-service usage
- LLM usage for route interpretation and planning assistance

### 6. Development Tools And Creative Software

- `$2,000`

This should cover:
- ChatGPT Pro or similar development support
- Adobe Creative Cloud
- premium software subscriptions needed for development and creative work

### 7. Advertising And Promotion Budget

- `$2,300`

This should cover:
- month 1 through month 3 launch ads
- small promoted-post tests
- search capture experiments
- light paid distribution once creatives are working organically

This is not a heavy ad campaign budget.
It is a careful founder-led launch budget.

### 8. Creative Production And Story Ads

- `$2,000`

This should cover:
- simple paid creative talent
- lightweight story-based ad production
- editing support
- stock / voiceover fallback assets

### 9. Freelance Support

- `$2,500`

This should cover:
- SEO or ASO help
- landing-page or analytics support
- occasional specialized contract work

### 10. Swag, Events, And Grassroots Promotion

- `$1,000`

This should cover:
- stickers
- QR-code handouts
- small event materials
- basic in-person promotion collateral

### 11. Contingency Buffer

- `$2,300`

This is the safety margin for:
- extra hosting
- extra API usage
- unplanned launch issues
- small business/admin costs

## Total

- `$42,000`

## Immediate Funding Ask Vs Total Funding Ask

There are really two asks:

### Initial Deposit

Minimum initial deposit:
- `$15,000`

That amount should cover:
- the full 12-week base developer pay
- enough operating cash to start product work cleanly

This is the minimum amount I would want deposited immediately to begin the build phase with
confidence.

### Total Planned Funding Ask

Total recommended funding commitment:
- `$42,000`

Preferred structure:
- one full capitalization up front

Acceptable fallback:
- an initial deposit of `$15,000`
- plus a written commitment to fund the remaining `$27,000` on an agreed schedule or milestone
  basis

The important point is:
- the ask is the full `$42,000`
- the `$15,000` number is only the first deposit

## Why The Immediate Ask Should Not Be Too Small

If the LLC is only lightly funded, it creates avoidable problems:
- execution risk rises
- compensation becomes uncertain
- launch expenses get delayed
- the company stays in an unstable half-funded state

That is exactly what should be avoided if the plan is to move fast.

## Revenue Offset Assumption

This funding model should not depend on early subscription revenue.

Any early revenue should be treated as:
- helpful extension of runway
- not something the plan depends on

This keeps the founder proposal cleaner and more realistic.

## Recommended Founder Proposal Language

Use language like:

"I want the full $42,000 funding commitment because that is what the actual plan requires from here
through release and 1,000 activated users. I want at least $15,000 funded into the LLC
immediately to begin the 12-week build phase, but I do not want us treating that as the whole ask.
That is only the first deposit."

## Bottom Line

If the question is:
"How much funding do we need from here to a released app plus 1,000 activated users?"

My recommendation is:
- `full funding commitment ask: $42,000`
- `minimum initial deposit: $15,000`
