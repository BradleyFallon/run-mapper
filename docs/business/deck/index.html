Opportunity

Why this product should exist

  • Generic map apps are not built for running route quality.
  • Run trackers help after the run, not before it.
  • Traveling runners and serious runners both have real route-selection pain.
  • The product should create the right route for you, not just show one somebody else uploaded.
Who it serves first Travelers, explorers, and technical runners
Core promise Get the run you would have planned for yourself if you had the time
Position Custom route scout, not generic mapping utility or route database

Product concept

How RouteScout works

1

Choose a Scout Spec

Users start from a planning spec like Safe Early Morning or Race Prep Hills.

2

Scout the best option

RouteScout evaluates multiple candidates against distance, terrain, surface, simplicity, route character, and the runner's actual priorities.

3

Save what is worth keeping

Users compare Route Cards, save favorites, and build a collection of routes that were originally created for them.

Timeline

12-week build plan

Weeks 1-2

Web planner MVP

Ship the planner, test with friends and early runners, and fix obvious workflow issues fast.

Weeks 3-4

Refine from feedback

Improve route trust, saved items, and planner clarity while starting the iPhone foundation.

Weeks 5-8

iPhone beta MVP

Release the app through beta, validate the full product flow, and stabilize subscriptions.

Weeks 9-12

Soft launch and traction

Move from beta to production, push lightly, and build toward the first activated-subscriber milestone.

Marketing plan

Phased founder-led growth

  • Start with friends, trusted runners, and small invited beta groups.
  • Use founder posts, route cards, and travel-running content before heavier ads.
  • Post selectively in running communities and travel-running crossover channels.
  • Increase promotion only after reliability, reviews, and onboarding are strong.

Business model

Cheap to try, premium enough to matter

  • Free use should be limited to basic route generation and product sampling.
  • Paid subscription unlocks ongoing planning depth, saved collections, and full mobile use.
  • Goal is to prove willingness to pay quickly with a simple subscription offer.
Free Try RouteScout

One route at a time, limited planning depth, and basic product sampling.

Paid: $4.99 / month Premium route planning

Intro offer: first 3 months at $0.99 per month. Beta testers get 6 months free.

Exit strategy

Build a premium route product that can stand alone or sell

  • Primary near-term goal is shipping and proving subscriber traction.
  • Longer-term strategic angle is acquisition interest from running, fitness, or mobility platforms.
  • Nike is a plausible long-term target if the product proves premium brand fit and strong iPhone execution.
Value driver Premium brand + differentiated route planning
