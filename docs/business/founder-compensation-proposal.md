# RouteScout Founder Compensation Proposal

## Purpose

This document proposes a compensation structure for the next RouteScout build phase.

It is meant to support a direct founder conversation about:
- labor
- risk
- fairness
- incentives

## Current Reality

The current operating reality is:
- one founder is financing and helping with business support
- one founder is doing most of the execution work

Current execution work includes:
- product design
- brand identity
- web app development
- iOS app development
- route system design
- launch execution
- marketing work

That means the labor split is not currently equivalent, even if equity is currently `50/50`.

## Proposal Summary

Requested near-term compensation:
- `$1,000/week`
- for `12 weeks`

Total:
- `$12,000`

Requested milestone bonuses:
- `$5,000` at `100 activated subscribers`
- `$10,000` at `1,000 activated subscribers`

## Why Up-Front Compensation Is Reasonable

Up-front compensation is reasonable because:
- the work is real, time-intensive labor
- the labor concentration is high on one side
- the company benefits immediately from that work
- equity alone does not solve short-term fairness when one founder is carrying most of execution

This weekly amount should be understood as:
- partial guaranteed compensation
- not full market-rate pay

## Why The Bonus Structure Makes Sense

The milestone bonuses are good because:
- they tie extra compensation to traction
- they align incentives with subscriber growth
- they reduce the need to push for a larger guaranteed weekly amount
- they make the proposal more business-oriented instead of purely salary-oriented

## Proposed Role Split

### Execution Founder

Primary responsibility:
- build and ship the product

Areas owned:
- product design
- brand
- UX
- frontend
- backend
- iOS app
- launch marketing execution

### Finance / Business Founder

Primary responsibility:
- finance and support the business side

Areas owned:
- company administration
- business support
- financing decisions
- founder support on strategic decisions

## Why This Matters

If one founder keeps `50%` of the company while contributing less execution labor during the build
phase, the other founder should not also be expected to absorb all short-term labor risk without
cash compensation.

This is the core fairness argument.

## Recommended Talking Points

In the founder conversation, keep the case simple:

1. the company needs a real build phase
2. that build phase depends heavily on your labor
3. the labor load is materially larger on your side
4. a modest weekly payment is fair and practical
5. subscriber bonuses keep incentives aligned

## Suggested Proposal Language

Use language like:

"For the next 12 weeks, I want to lead product, design, engineering, and launch execution and get
the planner live first, then the iPhone MVP. Since I’m carrying most of the execution work, I want
to be paid $1,000 per week during that build window. I also want milestone bonuses of $5,000 at
100 activated subscribers and $10,000 at 1,000 activated subscribers so the upside stays tied to
real traction."

That is clear and reasonable.

## What To Avoid Saying

Avoid framing it as:
- resentment about equity alone
- vague dissatisfaction
- "I’m doing everything and this is unfair" without a concrete proposal

Instead, frame it as:
- a structured build proposal
- a fair labor arrangement
- an execution plan tied to business milestones

## Recommendation

The cleanest founder proposal is:
- keep the equity discussion separate for now
- ask for a defined 12-week paid build phase
- make the work scope explicit
- keep the base amount modest but real
- use milestone bonuses to capture upside

## Important Note

This should eventually be documented in writing as part of founder agreements or company operating
documents.

This document is a planning proposal, not legal advice.
