# RouteScout Founder Term Sheet

## Purpose

This document is a practical founder term sheet for the next RouteScout build phase.

It is intended to help the founders align on:
- funding
- compensation
- ownership
- responsibilities
- milestones

This is not legal advice and not a final contract.
It is a negotiation and drafting document.

## Parties

- `Execution Founder`: product, design, engineering, brand, launch execution
- `Finance Founder`: financing, LLC support, business support
- `Company`: the LLC, once properly funded and operating

## Immediate Goal

Authorize a defined `12-week` build phase to ship:
- the web planner MVP first
- then the iPhone MVP

## Recommended Funding Structure

### Minimum Initial Funding

Recommended minimum LLC bank account funding before the build phase starts:
- `Option A: $12,000`
  Covers only the proposed 12 weeks of founder build compensation.
- `Option B: $15,000` recommended
  Covers compensation plus modest tools, hosting, App Store, and incidental costs.
- `Option C: $18,000`
  Gives more operating cushion.

Recommended founder proposal:
- ask for `at least $15,000` to be deposited into the LLC bank account before the build phase begins

Reason:
- your requested compensation alone totals `$12,000`
- the company should not run the build phase with no operating buffer

## Budget Use

The initial funded budget should be earmarked for:
- founder build compensation
- software tools
- hosting / infrastructure
- design assets and operating costs
- Apple developer / app distribution costs
- light testing and launch costs

The default rule should be:
- compensation is the primary budget use
- non-compensation spend above an agreed threshold requires founder approval

## Build Compensation

Requested compensation:
- `$1,000/week`
- for `12 weeks`

Total:
- `$12,000`

Payment terms:
- paid weekly from the LLC account
- payment date should be fixed in the agreement

## Milestone Bonuses

Requested bonuses:
- `$5,000` when the product reaches `100 activated subscribers`
- `$10,000` when the product reaches `1,000 activated subscribers`

The agreement should define:
- what counts as an activated subscriber
- what date the milestone is measured
- how quickly the bonus must be paid after the milestone is verified

Recommended definition:
- an activated subscriber is a user who starts a trial, paid subscription, or beta offer and
  actually uses the product

- launch execution
- marketing execution

### Finance Founder Responsibilities

## Ownership Principle

This is the most important section to get right.

Recommended principle:
- pre-existing IP remains owned by the creator who brought it in
- new RouteScout build-phase work is assigned to the LLC only if the agreed funding and payment
  conditions are satisfied

This protects the execution founder from doing unpaid build work while the company still claims full
ownership.

## Recommended IP Trigger Structure

Recommended clause concept:

1. all pre-existing materials remain the property of the creator
2. all new build-phase work product created by the execution founder remains the execution
   founder's property unless and until the LLC satisfies the agreed funding and payment conditions
3. once those conditions are satisfied, the agreed project IP is assigned to the LLC

This is much cleaner than vague statements about "we both own everything automatically."

## Recommended Funding Condition

Recommended ownership trigger:
- the LLC must first be funded with the agreed initial budget
- the LLC must continue making the agreed weekly payments when due

If those conditions are not met:
- no automatic assignment of newly created work occurs

## Recommended License Structure Before Full Assignment

If the founders want the company to still be able to review and test the work before full payment,
the contract can say:

- before full assignment, the LLC receives a limited, revocable internal evaluation license
- that license allows testing, review, and planning
- that license does not allow transfer, sale, sublicensing, or broad commercial exploitation

That is safer than giving away ownership up front.

## Practical IP Recommendation

Suggested founder position:
- "My pre-existing work stays mine."
- "New work becomes company IP only after the agreed funding and payment obligations are met."
- "Before that, the company can have a limited internal use license if needed."

## Repo, Accounts, And Asset Control

The agreement should also define control over:
- source code repositories
- Apple developer account
- domain names
- hosting accounts
- design files
- analytics accounts
- social and marketing accounts

Recommended rule:
- company-owned assets should be in company-controlled accounts once funded
- personal accounts used temporarily should be transferred or documented explicitly

## Expense Approval

Recommended rule:
- small operating expenses can be approved within the agreed budget
- larger unplanned expenses require approval from both founders

You should define a threshold number in the final agreement.

## Timeline

Initial term:
- `12 weeks`

Deliverables during that term:
- web planner MVP
- iPhone MVP
- launch and early subscriber testing

## Termination Logic

The agreement should define:
- what happens if funding is not deposited
- what happens if weekly payments stop
- what happens if either founder walks away
- what work product remains licensed or assigned in each scenario

Recommended protection:
- missed payment or failure to fund should pause or terminate the build obligation
- ownership assignment should not continue automatically if payment stops

## Subscriber Bonus Definitions

The contract should define:
- what counts as a subscriber
- whether trial users count
- whether refunded users count
- whether annual and monthly users both count

Recommended simple definition:
- an activated subscriber is a user who starts a trial, paid subscription, or beta offer and
  actually uses the product

Recommended reporting:
- report activated subscribers separately from paying subscribers

## Recommended Negotiation Position

Your clean founder position is:

1. approve a 12-week build phase
2. fund the LLC with at least `$15,000`
3. pay `$1,000/week` during the build phase
4. approve the subscriber milestone bonuses
5. keep pre-existing IP with the creator
6. assign newly created project IP to the LLC only after the agreed funding and payment conditions
   are satisfied

## Important Note

Copyright transfers and assignments should be done in signed writing.
Do not rely on informal oral understandings for ownership.
