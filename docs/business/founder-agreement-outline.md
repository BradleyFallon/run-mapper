# RouteScout Founder Agreement Outline

## Purpose

This document is a drafting outline for a founder agreement or founder build agreement.

It is not final contract language.
It is a structure for what the written agreement should cover.

## 1. Parties

- legal name of the LLC
- legal name of execution founder
- legal name of finance founder

## 2. Effective Date

- date the agreement becomes active

## 3. Purpose

State that the purpose of the agreement is to govern:
- the 12-week build phase
- founder roles
- funding
- compensation
- ownership of work product

## 4. Founder Roles

### Execution Founder

Responsible for:
- product design
- branding
- engineering
- web app build
- iOS app build
- launch execution
- marketing execution

### Finance Founder

Responsible for:
- funding the agreed budget
- supporting LLC business operations
- supporting company administration and business planning

## 5. Funding Obligation

Specify:
- exact amount to be deposited into the LLC bank account
- date by which it must be deposited
- whether funding is one deposit or staged deposits

Recommended structure:
- minimum initial deposit before build obligations begin

## 6. Approved Budget

Specify:
- what the build-phase budget is for
- what categories are approved

Recommended budget categories:
- developer pay
- hosting and software tools
- Apple developer and distribution fees
- marketing and launch basics

## 7. Weekly Compensation

Specify:
- `$1,000/week`
- for `12 weeks`
- payment method
- payment day

Also specify:
- whether missed payments pause work obligations

## 8. Activation Bonuses

Specify:
- `$5,000` at `100 activated users`
- `$10,000` at `1,000 activated users`

Define:
- what counts as an activated user
- who confirms the milestone
- how quickly the bonus is paid

Recommended working definition:
- an activated user is a user who creates an account, meaningfully uses RouteScout, and completes
  a real planning or run-use action

## 9. Pre-Existing IP

State clearly:
- each founder retains ownership of their pre-existing IP, tools, templates, libraries, brand
  concepts, and prior work unless separately assigned in writing

This should be explicit.

## 10. New Work Product During Build Phase

This is the key section.

Recommended structure:
- new work product created by the execution founder during the build phase is assigned to the LLC
  only if the agreed funding and payment conditions are met

Alternatively:
- assignment happens upon creation, but only after defined funding conditions are satisfied

Do not leave this vague.

## 11. Conditional Assignment Trigger

Define the assignment condition explicitly.

Example concept:
- assignment of newly created project IP occurs only after:
  - the initial funding amount has been deposited into the LLC account
  - the LLC remains current on required weekly payments

If those conditions fail:
- no further assignment occurs

## 12. Limited License Before Assignment

If desired, define a temporary license before full assignment:
- internal review and testing only
- no transfer
- no sublicensing
- no sale
- revocable if payment obligations fail

## 13. Repositories, Accounts, And Assets

Define ownership and control of:
- code repositories
- domain names
- Apple developer account
- hosting accounts
- design files
- social accounts
- analytics and marketing accounts

Specify which are:
- company-owned
- temporarily founder-controlled
- required to be transferred later

## 14. Expense Approval

Define:
- which expenses can be approved unilaterally
- which expenses need both founders to approve
- the threshold for special approval

## 15. Milestones And Deliverables

State the intended build-phase goals:
- web planner MVP
- iPhone MVP
- launch-ready activation test

These should be framed as business goals, not guaranteed outcomes.

## 16. Term And Review Point

Define:
- initial `12-week` term
- review meeting at the end of the term

At review:
- continue
- extend
- revise
- stop

## 17. Termination

Define what happens if:
- funding is not deposited
- weekly payments stop
- a founder stops performing
- the founders mutually stop the project

This section should also define:
- post-termination IP status
- post-termination license status
- repository and account access

## 18. Confidentiality

Define standard confidentiality obligations for:
- source code
- designs
- business plans
- user and product data

## 19. Founder Authority

Define which decisions require:
- one founder
- both founders
- LLC approval

This matters especially for:
- spending
- major partnerships
- sale discussions
- IP transfers

## 20. Dispute Resolution

The final contract should define:
- governing law
- venue
- dispute resolution process

Do not skip this in the final version.

## 21. Entire Agreement / Amendments

State that:
- amendments must be in writing
- informal side understandings do not override the written agreement

## 22. Signature Block

Final agreement should include:
- founder signatures
- date
- LLC signatory if appropriate

## Recommended Drafting Note

When this gets turned into a real agreement:
- keep ownership clauses extremely clear
- keep payment triggers explicit
- avoid relying on vague "we'll work it out later" language
