# RouteScout Founder Proposal Summary

## Goal

Agree on a 3-month build phase for RouteScout with:
- clear scope
- clear compensation
- clear milestones

## Product Goal

Build RouteScout as:
- a planning-first web app
- followed by an iPhone MVP

Immediate focus:
- get the planner web app up as soon as possible

Then:
- complete the iPhone MVP in the following weeks

## Why This Makes Sense

RouteScout has a credible niche:
- runners planning in unfamiliar places
- runners who want technically suitable routes
- runners who want a premium product experience
- runners who want a route created for them, not just a route looked up from someone else

The product already has:
- product design direction
- brand identity
- planner information architecture
- style guide
- route scoring model
- route card and badge system

The core product angle is now clear:
- RouteScout scouts out the route for the runner
- it is not primarily a route-sharing library
- it aims to replace manual map research with a custom route recommendation

So the next phase is execution, not vague ideation.

## Proposed 12-Week Build Phase

### Deliverables

- working web planner MVP
- saved plans and route generation
- iPhone MVP
- early launch and activation testing

### Compensation

- `$1,000/week`
- for `12 weeks`

Total:
- `$12,000`

### Bonuses

- `$5,000` at `100 activated users`
- `$10,000` at `1,000 activated users`

Activated user definition:
- a user who creates an account, meaningfully uses RouteScout, and completes a real planning or
  run-use action

## Founder Role Split

### Your role

- product
- design
- engineering
- brand
- launch execution
- marketing execution

### Co-founder role

- financing
- business support
- company support

## Why Compensation Is Fair

The execution burden is not evenly split.

So the proposal is:
- keep equity as-is for now
- add a modest paid build phase
- tie additional upside to activation milestones

That is a practical way to handle a 50/50 company when one founder is carrying most of the
execution work in the near term.

## Ask

Approve:

1. a `12-week` build phase
2. `$1,000/week` during that phase
3. the milestone bonus structure
4. a full funding commitment of `$42,000` through release and `1,000` activated users
5. an initial deposit of at least `$15,000` into the LLC to start cleanly
6. a checkpoint at the end of the 12 weeks to decide what happens next

## Next Decision After 12 Weeks

At the end of the build phase, decide whether to:
- continue funding
- keep iterating lean
- pursue partnerships or acquisition interest
- stop if traction is weak
