# RouteScout Six-Month Spend Timeline

## Purpose

This document shows a practical month-by-month spending plan for the first `6 months`.

It is meant to answer:
- how cash should be used over time
- when the major spending categories hit
- what the company should be trying to achieve by month `6`

## Important Framing

Month `6` should be treated as:
- a target for being ready for strategic conversations
- a point where RouteScout is actively trying to open partnership or acquisition conversations
- a point where the product, brand, and early traction could make RouteScout interesting to a
  larger sports or fitness brand

Month `6` should **not** be treated as a guaranteed acquisition close.

The right planning stance is:
- by month `6`, RouteScout should be polished enough and validated enough that we are actively
  trying to sell, partner, or open serious strategic conversations with a major sports brand

## Planning Assumptions

This timeline assumes:
- `12 weeks` of active developer pay at `$1,000/week`
- web planner first
- iPhone beta next
- public launch during the first `3 months`
- months `4-6` focused on refinement, growth, and positioning
- milestone bonuses are budgeted as reserves, not assumed to be paid on a fixed month

## Month-6 Strategic Target

By the end of month `6`, RouteScout should aim to have:
- a credible web planner
- a launched iPhone product
- early user traction
- functioning billing
- early reviews and user stories
- strong brand presentation
- a believable case for partnership or acquisition interest
- founder materials ready for serious outreach
- an active attempt underway to start conversations with a major sports brand

That is the right month-6 goal.

The goal is not:
- assume a big sports brand has already bought the company

The goal is:
- be strong enough that those conversations become realistic and worth pursuing immediately

## Suggested Founder Language

Use language like:

"I think we should lay out spending across six months so we know what the actual path looks like.
The first three months are heavy build months, then the next three are for launch, refinement, and
growth. By month six, I want us ready and actively trying for partnership or acquisition
conversations with a major sports brand. I am not saying that outcome is guaranteed by then, but I
do think we should plan to give it a serious shot."
