# RouteScout One-Page Brief

## What RouteScout Is

RouteScout is a premium running route planning product.

It helps a runner answer a simple question:

"I am here, I have this much time, and I want this kind of run. What should I do?"

The core idea is that RouteScout does not mainly depend on showing routes that other people already
uploaded. Its value is that it scouts out a custom route for the runner based on:
- location
- target distance or time
- surface preferences
- elevation preferences
- navigation confidence
- atmosphere and route character

It is meant to feel like an experienced runner studied the map, weighed the tradeoffs, and picked
the best option for that specific moment.

## Why It Matters

Planning a good run is often harder than it sounds.

That is especially true when the runner:
- is traveling
- is new to an area
- wants a specific workout
- wants to explore without getting lost
- wants a route that feels worth the effort before they leave the house or hotel

Generic maps are not built for running route quality.
Run trackers are mostly useful after the run, not before it.
Route-sharing products depend heavily on what someone else happened to upload.

RouteScout is different because it aims to create the right route for this runner, here and now.

## Product Shape

RouteScout is designed as:
- a web planner first
- followed by a full iPhone product

The web product is the fastest way to ship useful route planning.
The iPhone app is the full product surface for review, route use, and subscription value.

The product direction is premium:
- dark, high-contrast visual identity
- calm, athletic feel
- strong Apple- and Nike-compatible brand presentation

## Near-Term Plan

The near-term plan is a focused `12-week` build phase inside a broader `6-month` push.

### First 12 Weeks

- ship the web planner MVP
- test with friends and invited early users
- improve the planner from feedback
- build and release the iPhone MVP through beta
- soft-launch and begin early user growth

### By Month 6

The goal is to have:
- a launched web planner
- a launched iPhone app
- functioning billing
- early activated users
- strong product and brand presentation
- enough proof to actively pursue partnership or acquisition conversations with a major sports
  brand

The goal is not to assume an acquisition is guaranteed by month 6.
The goal is to be ready and actively trying.

## Business Plan And Funding

The founder proposal is:
- `$1,000/week` for `12 weeks`
- `$5,000` bonus at `100 activated users`
- `$10,000` bonus at `1,000 activated users`

Total planned funding ask:
- `$42,000`

Minimum initial deposit:
- `$15,000`

The logic is simple:
- this is real full-time build work
- the execution burden is heavily on the product/design/engineering side
- if one founder is keeping `50%` primarily for financing, then the company should be properly
  funded for the real plan, not only for the first few weeks

## Bottom Line

RouteScout is a premium custom route-scouting product for runners.

Its job is to replace guesswork and manual map research with a route that feels thoughtfully chosen
for the runner's real needs.

The plan is to ship quickly, prove demand, build a credible brand, and push toward early traction
and strategic interest within the first 6 months.
